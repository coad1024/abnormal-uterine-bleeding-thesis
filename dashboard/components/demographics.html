Patient Demographics

This section visualizes the distribution of age, presenting complaints, parity, and hormonal drug history among the 351 patients studied.

Age Distribution (Fig. 2)

Peak incidence of AUB observed in women aged 31-40 years. Most cases appear in reproductive age group.

Parity Distribution (Fig. 3)

Majority of cases occurred in multiparous women, suggesting a correlation between parity and endometrial abnormalities.

Presenting Complaints (Fig. 4)

Heavy menstrual bleeding was the most common presenting complaint, followed by irregular bleeding patterns.

Drug History (Fig. 5)

The majority of patients had no significant hormonal drug history, while oral contraceptives were the most common among those with drug exposure.
